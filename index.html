? Why AOT uses stake share but JIT uses total market

For AOT blockspace auctions, Raiku Stake % represents the share of total Solana stake delegated to Raiku-connected validators, because AOT capacity depends on validator stake share and reserved blockspace access.

JIT is modeled differently: it starts from an observed market benchmark and annualizes revenue, similar to MEV tip-based benchmark extrapolations. In this model, total Solana stake share is a direct driver for AOT, but not the direct entry point for JIT.

JIT Market Assumption & Methodology
Active JIT market
— data-driven default
Annualized JIT tip market used as model input. Set by the active preset or the JIT Total Market slider.
Other non-Raiku JIT — treatment in this model
The total JIT market includes two distinct flows on covered stake:
• Raiku JIT — tips routed through Raiku's ordering protocol. Subject to protocol take rate, customer rebate, and treasury split.
• Other non-Raiku JIT — tips from competing ordering services (e.g. direct Jito bundles) that bypass Raiku protocol take entirely and flow straight to validators.

Other non-Raiku JIT is automatically derived as max(Raiku Stake % − Raiku JIT Market Share, 0). The logic: Raiku's covered stake creates a JIT opportunity ceiling; the portion not captured by Raiku's protocol flows to validators via other ordering services. It is included in validator-side revenue but excluded from Raiku gross and protocol treasury. No double-counting is possible by construction.

Protocol Take Rates: AOT and JIT each use their own slider (default 5%). Validator base = 1 – take rate for that stream. Rebates and validator bonus are funded from the corresponding protocol pool (= gross × stream take rate), never from gross revenue directly.
Two-waterfall model: AOT and JIT streams are calculated independently, then aggregated. Validator bonus applies only to AOT. If either take rate = 0%, redistributions on that stream are forced to 0.

Methodology & Data Sources
Data Source
Input artifact: data/aot_programs.v1.js (produced upstream from canonical revenue-model outputs)
Schema: aot_programs.v1
Period: 30-day window from artifact metadata
Programs: Artifact rows with CU > 0 (core: aot + both, separate arbitrage-bot layer: jit where available)
Aggregation Method
Fee/CU values are CU-weighted averages across programs in each frozen group. Programs consuming more compute units have proportionally more influence on group metrics.
non-base fee/CU = Σ(priority + mev_jit) × 10⁹ / Σ(CU)
total fee/CU = Σ(base + priority + mev_jit) × 10⁹ / Σ(CU)
Proxy Framing
These are current observed fees from the loaded artifact. The default analytical lens is non-base fees (priority + MEV / JIT), with total fees (base + priority + MEV / JIT) shown as comparison. They serve as a proxy for willingness-to-pay in a future AOT auction, not literal AOT bid prices.
Category Mapping
Runtime groups consume segment_key directly from the artifact contract and follow the frozen UI structure.
Core groups: prop_amm, cranker, oracle, depin, payments, dex (aggregator + amm_family + orderbook), lending, perps, bridge, nft, gaming. Separate arbitrage-bot layer: arb_bot (when JIT rows exist).
⏱ Temporal Data Coverage
Network-level temporal data (available): ~100 epochs (Aug 2025 – Mar 2026) with per-epoch priority fees (SOL), MEV rewards (SOL), SOL price (USD). Source: D.e embedded dataset.

Per-program temporal data (not loaded): The current simulator contract intentionally loads only 30-day aggregate AOT program metrics from data/aot_programs.v1.js.

Per-category charts: Category-level daily Fee/CU and daily CU charts will be enabled when a dedicated upstream daily artifact is provided.

Status: Network-level backdrop charts remain available from D.e. AOT per-category temporal curves are intentionally disabled in this build.
