Scenario assumptions — market regimes

The Base Case uses the data-driven default computed from recent normal-activity epochs. The Conservative scenario assumes a lower-activity market (e.g. reduced MEV during quiet periods).

The Optimistic and Bull Case assumptions are informed by historically stronger congestion and peak MEV periods — such as memecoin surges and high on-chain trading volume — that we identified in our research on Solana network activity.

Note: Richer integration of peak-period data is still a work in progress. The optimistic and bullish values are already conceptually anchored to those higher-activity regimes, but may be refined as more granular data becomes available.

Priority fee/CU = incremental bid above the mandatory base fee (5000 lam/signature). Reflects marginal willingness to pay for transaction priority.
Total fee/CU = base + priority fees combined. Reflects the full revealed willingness to pay for blockspace — the reservation price.

Key fact: Prop AMM / high-fee customers represent a small share of CU volume but generate the majority of AOT-relevant fee revenue on the current Solana network.

How the scenario fee/CU values are determined

Each scenario represents a different customer acquisition outcome and a different market pressure regime. The key insight is that the relevant fee metric itself shifts as demand for AOT blockspace increases:

Low demand (Conservative / Base): Auction bids are anchored to priority fee/CU — the incremental cost above the mandatory base fee. In this regime, blockspace is abundant and bids reflect marginal willingness to pay. Observed: low-fee DeFi ~— lam/CU, Prop AMMs ~— lam/CU.
Transition (Optimistic): As Prop AMM share grows and blockspace becomes scarcer, auction bids move above marginal priority fees toward the full reservation price. Bids reflect increasing competition, not just priority-fee logic.
High demand (Bull): Fully competitive market — bids converge on total fee/CU, the full revealed willingness to pay. Observed: Prop AMMs ~— lam/CU. This is the theoretical ceiling where fee = reservation price.

In summary: the scenario ladder spans from conservative priority-fee-based estimates to aggressive total-fee-based valuations of AOT blockspace. Both metrics are real, observed on-chain — the question is which regime applies given the competitive pressure in each scenario.

Aggregation Method
Fee/CU values are CU-weighted averages across all programs in each business category. Programs consuming more compute units have proportionally more influence on the category's fee/CU.
fee/CU = Σ(total_fees) × 10⁹ / Σ(CU)
Proxy Framing
These are current observed fees (priority + base) paid by Solana programs under today's fee market. They serve as a proxy for willingness-to-pay in a future AOT auction — not literal AOT bid prices. Actual clearing prices will depend on auction design, competition dynamics, and blockspace scarcity.
